Population in Tokyo Between 2010 to 2020

Does Population Decrease for All Locations?

Information

One of the three effective narrative visualization structures : Interactive Slideshow
Users can click the buttons on the top to see other detailed information, such as difference among
2010-2020, 3 other locations from 2010 to 2010, and the total difference in the whole country.

As users go into next pages, they will be able to see the population in 2010, 2015, and 2020 by state.
If user would like to review other states’ population, he or she can select the location names on the buttons
in the top menu.

Messaging:

Some may have heard it before, but the total population in Japan has been decreasing every year since early
2000s. Its government prefects its population would continue to drop by a little bit every year. However, I
was curious and wanted to communicate the message of population change and trends for certain states. Japan
has 47 states (called prefectures), and does every state decrease its population since total population has
been decreasing? The answer is no. There are 7 states have increased population this past 10 years, which
users would be able to see in “Changes by State”.

Narrative Structure: Interactive Slideshow

Users can follow the buttons from left to right to follow the “steps” as it goes to the next pages.
This is interactive slideshow because there are a little bit “drill-down” pages to narrow down or provide details of
certain states’ population.

Visual Structure:

Each scene is followed by bar graph, and the structure is contestant across this narrative visualization
project. Viewers can understand the data by reading the texts in X and Y axis, and there are descriptions in
the title. There are also tooltips and annotations for users to follow, so they can stay focused on the
presented data on each scene.
There are 8 buttons on the top of each html page, and viewers can follow the buttons from the left to right
along with descriptions

Scenes:

Scenes contain 8 buttons on the top of the page and one chart underearth the options. Scenes are followed in
a consistent format, which is options on the top and single bar chart and the same way as other scenes to
avoid viewers to get confused.

The annotation template contains texts and pointers to pinpoint the location that I would like to emphasize
in the bar graph. The annotations especially support certain data points. For example, I used one for the
last bar graph to deliver the message where the population has been decreasing and it pointed the location
of the bar graph, so users are easily able to follow and focus the message I would like to communicate. Some
annotations change since the trends are changed and I would like to put emphasis on the increased or
decreased values.

Parameters:

My parameters are variables, which is chart years in populations, and animated bar charts and top of options
as slide numbers. Viewers can see the parameters on the top of each page.

Triggers:

My triggers are used a connector to the next chart or page to change the parameters. My triggers are buttons
and hovering over the bar graph to display the details information.
